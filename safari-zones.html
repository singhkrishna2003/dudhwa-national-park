Explore Dudhwa's Wildlife Zones

Discover the diverse safari zones of Dudhwa Tiger Reserve, each offering unique landscapes and unforgettable wildlife encounters.

Dudhwa Tiger Reserve is divided into distinct safari zones, each offering unique wildlife experiences and landscapes. Plan your safari across these zones for the best chance of spotting diverse species.

🟢 Dudhwa Safari Zone

Location: Main tourism hub near Palia Kalan

Highlights:

  • Spectacular open grasslands ideal for wildlife viewing
  • Excellent tiger sightings
  • Asian elephant encounters
  • Beautiful sal forest trails

This is the most popular zone, known for its expansive grasslands where herbivores gather, attracting predators. The zone offers excellent opportunities to observe natural predator-prey dynamics.

Best For: Tiger tracking, elephant sightings, photography

🟤 Sonaripur Safari Zone

Location: Within Dudhwa National Park

Highlights:

  • One-horned rhinoceros conservation area
  • Rhino enclosure with 27 sq km fenced habitat
  • Over 46 rhinos residing here
  • Walking safari opportunities

Sonaripur is dedicated to rhino conservation and offers the rare opportunity to observe these prehistoric giants up close. The enclosure allows visitors to safely view rhinos in a semi-natural environment.

Best For: Rhino viewing, conservation education

🟢 Kishanpur Wildlife Sanctuary

Location: 15 km from Dudhwa, near Mailani

Highlights:

  • Diverse tiger population
  • Exceptional birdwatching with 300+ species
  • Leopard sightings
  • Sloth bear habitat
  • Peaceful forest environment

Kishanpur offers a more tranquil safari experience compared to the main Dudhwa zone. Its mixed forest and grassland habitat supports rich biodiversity, making it ideal for serious wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.

Best For: Birdwatching, leopard spotting, peaceful nature walks

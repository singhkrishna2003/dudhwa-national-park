Plan Your Visit to Dudhwa

Everything you need to know before visiting Dudhwa National Park — from best seasons to safari timings and essential guidelines.

Park Timings

Open: November 15 to June 15 annually

Closed during monsoon season (July–October) for animal breeding and forest regeneration.

Best Time to Visit

November to February (Peak Season)

  • Pleasant weather (10–25°C)
  • Best wildlife sightings
  • Ideal for families and international tourists
  • Higher accommodation rates

March to April (Excellent Season)

  • Warm but comfortable (20–35°C)
  • Excellent tiger sightings near waterholes
  • Great for photography
  • Moderate crowd levels

May to Mid-June (Summer Season)

  • Hot weather (30–40°C)
  • Maximum wildlife concentration near water sources
  • Best for serious wildlife enthusiasts
  • Lower tourist numbers

Note: Carry warm clothing for winter visits (December–February) as temperatures can drop significantly at night.

Safari Timings

Winter Season (November 1 – March 31)

Morning Safari: 6:30 AM – 10:00 AM

Evening Safari: 3:00 PM – Sunset

Summer Season (April 1 – June 15)

Morning Safari: 6:00 AM – 9:30 AM

Evening Safari: 4:30 PM – Sunset

Morning safaris offer the best chances for wildlife sightings as animals are most active during cooler hours.

Entry Requirements

Documents Required

  • Valid government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, Driving License)
  • Same ID must be presented at the park entrance

Entry Fees (Subject to Change)

  • Indian Nationals: ₹200 per person
  • Foreign Nationals: ₹1,600 per person
  • Vehicle Entry: ₹100 per vehicle
  • Still Camera: Usually included
  • Video Camera: Additional charges may apply

Important Guidelines

Do’s

  • Arrive 15 minutes before safari time
  • Follow guide instructions at all times
  • Maintain silence during wildlife viewing
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing
  • Carry water, snacks, and sun protection
  • Bring binoculars

Don’ts

  • Do not get down from the vehicle
  • No loud noises or music
  • No littering inside the park
  • No smoking or alcohol
  • No feeding animals
  • No bright or shiny clothing
